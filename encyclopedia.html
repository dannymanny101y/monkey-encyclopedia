Marmosets

Marmosets are the smallest species of monkeys and inhabit the upper canopy layer. They are a whopping 5 inches tall and are highly active and energetic. Tamarins

Tamarins inhabit the tropical rainforests, and are mainly found in Brazil. Their colors vary in shades of black, brown, white, and bright orange. Tamarins with brown and white fur are known as "Emperor Tamarins", while ones with bright orange fur are "Golden Lion Tamarins". These creatures are omnivores.

Squirrel Monkeys

Squirrel monkeys are found in Central and South American forests. They are 25-35 cm tall and live in the canopy layer of trees. They vary in color from yellowish orange and olive-colored. They are found in large, united groups of about 100-300 monkeys of their kind.

Lesula

Guinea Baboons

The Guinea Baboon is native to the west side of Africa and is one of the smallest species in the Baboon species. It's diet consists of greens, grass, fruits, young birds, invertebrates, and small mammals.

Capuchins

One of the more tame monkey species, the Capuchins are usually less tempermental and are the most recommended monkey breed to keep as a pet. Growing over 2 feet tall, these adorable monkeys are usually white, brown, or black and are omnivores.

Howler Monkeys

Also know as the laziest monkeys, the Howler monkeys are infamous for being able to sleep for over 15 hours and are known to stay in their dwelling for long periods of time. They inhabit the tropical forests of Central and South America, and are the largest group of primates in the New World.

The name "wooly" represents their thick fur, which keeps them warm. Ther fur color consists from light grey to black and they inhabit the tropical rainforests in North and South America. On average, they weigh a little over a standard bowling ball(39 lbs). They are omnivores, eat both meat and vegetables, and move in large groups for protection and company.
